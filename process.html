Chapter 04 · The way we work

Six steps,
written down.

Building a house is the largest financial decision most families ever make. We have tried, over twenty-two years, to make the process less opaque. This is exactly how a project moves through our studio.

01
Week 0 — A long first conversation

Listen.

Free, unhurried, on a Saturday — at our studio or, better, on your land. We listen. You don't need to bring a brief; you bring a story.

  • What you want (and don't want)
  • Budget honest, not hopeful
  • Your family, your routines, your ghosts
  • A walk through your land if it exists
Outcome — a one-page brief, written by us, signed off by you.
02
Weeks 1 – 3 — The land speaks first

Survey.

Before we draw a line, we measure. Topography, soil, sun path through the year, neighbours, water, vehicular approach, panchayat regulations.

  • Topographical & site survey
  • Soil bearing test
  • Sun-path & wind diagrams
  • Statutory & vastu review
Outcome — site dossier (~30 pages), the foundation of every drawing that follows.
03
Weeks 4 – 8 — Three options on paper

Sketch.

We present three concept directions, all sketched by hand, all costed roughly. Different floor plans, different orientations, different roof languages. We sit together. We talk. You choose, or we mix.

  • Three concept options, hand sketched
  • Massing model in foam & ply
  • Indicative cost band per option
  • Two rounds of revisions on the chosen direction
Outcome — a frozen concept design, signed off, with a cost envelope you trust.
04
Weeks 9 – 18 — Drawings the mason can read

Detail.

Working drawings, structure, electrical, plumbing, joinery, finishes, landscape. Itemised quantity statement. Approvals filed in parallel.

  • Full architectural & structural drawing set
  • MEP and joinery details
  • Specifications book
  • Bill of quantities — to the bag, to the brick
  • Panchayat / municipal approvals
Outcome — a frozen drawing set and a cost that lands within ±4% of the final bill.
05
Months 5 – 18 — Hands and time

Build.

Our crew on site. An architect on site weekly. Castings overseen. Monthly billing. A short Saturday update, every week, by message — what was done, what's next, what's costed.

  • In-house masons, carpenters, electricians, plumbers
  • Weekly architect site visit, fortnightly principal visit
  • Monthly itemised bill, photographs attached
  • You can walk the site any day, no notice required
Outcome — a finished building, on schedule (±6 weeks), within ±4% of estimate.
06
Day 0 of living — and onward

Hand over.

Snag list. Cleanup. The maintenance book, written by us, annotated with the names & numbers of every person who worked on your home. One year of free defect liability. A monsoon walk-through after the first rains.

  • Annotated maintenance book
  • Snag list resolved before keys
  • One-year defects liability
  • Post-monsoon walkthrough
Outcome — a building you can live in, repair, and one day pass on.
— Our promise

Three numbers we hold ourselves to.

±4%

Final cost vs. our written estimate. Tracked across every project we've completed since 2014.

±6 wk

Final handover vs. our written schedule. Monsoons and government delays excluded — and even then, mostly held.

1 yr

Free defects liability after handover. Monsoon walk-through included. We answer your call before we answer a new client's.
